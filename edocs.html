Sending and Receiving Documents by Email Need help? Ask Chatty Cathy →

Email allows you to send and receive important documents quickly, such as forms, photos, or letters. This is a common way to share information with doctors, businesses, and family members. In this lesson, you will learn how to send and open documents safely.

Video #1: How to Attach a File to an Email
The top video explains how to attach a file to an email in just a few easy steps. This quick tutorial works for Gmail, Outlook, and most email apps on computer or mobile devices.

Video #2: How to Open and Save an Email Attachment
The video on the bottom explains how to download and save an email attachment on both computers and mobile devices.

Why is sending documents with email important?
A document can refer to any file on your device. Common file types include photos, videos, or PDFs. Knowing how to send and receive documents through email communications can help you stay in the loop. Many companies and businesses have shifted to email communications over the last couple of years. A lot of modern documents only exist digitally, making email a great way to share them with others.

To send a document follow these steps:

  1. Open your email → Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo, etc.
  2. Click Compose or New Email
  3. Enter the recipient's email address
  4. Click the paperclip icon → this will 'attach' the document
  5. Choose the document you want to send from your device
  6. Wait for it to upload
  7. Click Send

Before you send, take a moment to check you selected the correct document and entered the correct recipient email.

For receiving a document from someone else:

  1. Open the email
  2. Look for an attachment → shown as the file name at the top or bottom of the email
  3. Click the file to open it
  4. Download to your computer if needed

★ Important Tip ★
Only open attachments if you know the sender or were expecting the document. If you are unsure, do not open it.
