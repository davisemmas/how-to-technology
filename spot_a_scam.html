What is a scam?
Scams (or fraud) are messages or calls designed to trick people into giving away money or personal information. They can happen to anyone, and they are becoming more common online. In this lesson, you will learn how to recognize scams and protect yourself.

Below are common warning signs to look out for:

  • Unexpected messages → such as a random email or bill
  • Strange payment requests → gift cards, cryptocurrency (BitCoin)
  • Requests for personal information → bank details, passwords
  • Urgency → "Act now or your account will be closed!"

What if I'm suspicious, but unsure if a message is a scam?
If you recieve a message and can't tell if it is a scam:

  1. Stop and do not respond
  2. Do not click any links or download any attachments
  3. Ask a trusted friend or family member to take a look

If a message says it is from a company, check the company's official website or help line for more information. Real companies will never ask for your account information or pressure you to act immediately.

★ Important Tip ★
Look out for unknown email addresses, spelling errors, and messages about recovering "lost" accounts. If you think something is suspicious, do not click on any links or provide personal infromation like your phone number, financial information, or social security number.
